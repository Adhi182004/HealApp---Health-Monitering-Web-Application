Personal Finance Basics
Foundation rules every Indian must know
💡 Core Money Rules
📐
50-30-20 Rule
Spend 50% on needs (rent, food, bills), 30% on wants (dining, entertainment), 20% on savings and investments. Adapt to 60-20-20 if your city has high rent.
🛡
Emergency Fund First
Keep 6 months of expenses in a liquid fund or high-interest savings account before investing anywhere. This prevents panic selling during emergencies.
💳
Kill Bad Debt First
Credit card debt at 36–42% annual interest is wealth destruction. Pay it off before any investment. No market returns consistently beat 36% cost of debt.
📊
Net Worth Over Income
Track your net worth (assets minus liabilities) monthly — not just salary. A ₹2L/month earner who saves ₹50K beats a ₹5L earner who saves nothing.
⏱
Start Early — Compounding Is Real
₹5,000/month from age 25 grows to ~₹3.5 Cr at 12% by age 60. Starting at 35 gives only ~₹1 Cr. 10 years costs ₹2.5 Cr.

📋 Other Sections
🏥
80D — Health Insurance
₹25,000 for self/family. Additional ₹25,000 for parents. If parents are senior citizens, limit is ₹50,000. Total possible: ₹75,000 deduction.
🎓
80E — Education Loan
Full interest deduction on education loan for 8 years. No upper limit. Covers loans for self, spouse or children.
🏠
24(b) — Home Loan Interest
Up to ₹2 lakh deduction on home loan interest for self-occupied property. No limit if property is rented out.
🏦
80CCD(1B) — Extra NPS
Additional ₹50,000 deduction over 80C limit by investing in NPS. Total possible tax saving from NPS: ₹2 lakh.
⚖ Old vs New Tax Regime
📊
New Regime (Default from 2024)
Lower slab rates. No deductions (80C, 80D etc. not available). Best for those with few investments or HRA not applicable.
📋
Old Regime
Higher slab rates but all deductions apply. Better if you claim 80C (₹1.5L) + 80D + HRA + Home loan interest.
🧮
Break-Even Rule
If your total deductions exceed ₹3.75 lakh (for 30% bracket), old regime saves more. Calculate before choosing every year.

🧱 Core & Satellite Model
Divide your portfolio into stable core (70–80%) and high-growth satellite (20–30%).
🏛
Core (70%) — Stability
Index funds, large-cap mutual funds, PPF, debt funds. Low risk, steady compounding.
🚀
Satellite (30%) — Growth
Mid-cap/small-cap funds, direct stocks, sectoral bets, international funds.
📊 Age-Based Asset Allocation
Equity % Rule
Equity % = 100 − Your Age
At 25 → 75% equity, 25% debt
At 45 → 55% equity, 45% debt
At 60 → 40% equity, 60% debt
Rebalance once a year. Don't chase returns — stick to your allocation.

SIP & Mutual Fund Strategy
The most powerful wealth-building tool for salaried Indians
🔄 How SIP Works
SIP Future Value
FV = P × [((1+r)ⁿ − 1) / r] × (1+r)
P = monthly amount · r = monthly rate · n = months

₹10,000/month @ 12% for 20 years = ₹98 Lakhs
📅
Never Stop SIP in Crashes
Market crashes are SIP's best friend — you buy more units at lower prices. Stopping kills the compounding.
⬆
Step-Up SIP Annually
Increase SIP by 10% every year matching salary hike. ₹5K SIP stepped up becomes ₹5.5K → ₹6K → exponential growth.
📊 Fund Categories
🏔
Large Cap
Top 100 companies by market cap. Stable. 10–13% returns. Best for conservative equity investors. Example: HDFC Top 100.
⚡
Mid Cap
101–250 rank companies. Higher risk, higher reward. 14–18% potential. Example: Nippon India Mid Cap.
🚀
Small Cap
Below 250 rank. Very high risk, very high reward. 3–5 year minimum. Example: SBI Small Cap Fund.
📊
Index Fund
Tracks Nifty 50 or Sensex. Lowest expense ratio (0.1%). Beats most active funds over 10 years. Example: UTI Nifty 50 Index.
⚖
Hybrid / Balanced
Mix of equity + debt. Lower volatility. Best for 3–5 year goals. Example: HDFC Balanced Advantage Fund.
🔍 How to Pick a Fund
📉
Check Expense Ratio
Lower is better. Active funds: under 1%. Index funds: under 0.2%. Over 2% is a red flag — avoid.
📅
Minimum 5-Year Track Record
Only judge a fund by rolling returns over 5+ years — not 1-year rankings. Check if it beat the benchmark consistently.
🏦
Check AUM Size
Very small AUM (under ₹500 Cr) = risky. Too large AUM in mid/small cap = difficult to buy quality stocks. Sweet spot: ₹1,000–₹10,000 Cr for active funds.
⚠
Don't Chase Recent Top Performers
Last year's #1 fund rarely repeats. This is called "Recency Bias" — one of the most expensive investing mistakes.

Advanced Investment Theory
Frameworks that separate informed investors from the crowd
🧠 Behavioural Finance Traps
🪞
Loss Aversion
Humans feel losses 2x more painfully than equivalent gains. This causes panic selling at the bottom — the worst time to sell.
📰
Confirmation Bias
We seek news that confirms what we already believe about a stock. Actively read opposing views before any investment.
🐑
Herd Mentality
Buying because "everyone is buying" (crypto in 2021, small caps in 2024). When your auto-rickshaw driver gives stock tips — it's the top.
⚓
Anchoring Bias
Holding a fallen stock hoping it "comes back to your buy price." The stock doesn't know what you paid. Judge on current fundamentals.
📐 Key Investment Theories
📊
Efficient Market Hypothesis (EMH)
Stock prices reflect all available information at all times. If true, beating the market consistently is impossible — hence index funds outperform most active funds long-term.
🥧
Modern Portfolio Theory (MPT)
Diversification reduces risk without sacrificing return. Correlation matters — combine assets that don't move together (equity + gold + bonds).
💰
Time Value of Money
₹1 lakh today is worth more than ₹1 lakh 5 years from now due to inflation and opportunity cost. Always calculate Present Value (PV) of future money.
📉
Dollar Cost Averaging (DCA)
Invest fixed amount regularly regardless of market level. SIP is DCA. Removes timing risk entirely — you buy cheap when markets fall automatically.

Common Financial Mistakes
What most Indians do wrong — and how to avoid them
🚫 Avoid These
🚫
Mixing Insurance with Investment
LIC endowment / money-back plans give 4–5% returns and call it "safe." Buy pure term insurance (₹1 Cr cover for ₹10,000/year) and invest the rest in mutual funds separately.
🚫
No Term Insurance
If you have dependents and no term life insurance, your family is financially unprotected. ₹1 Cr term cover at age 30 costs only ~₹8,000–10,000 per year.
🚫
FD for Long-Term Wealth
FD at 7% with 30% tax bracket = 4.9% effective return. Inflation at 6% = you're actually losing purchasing power. Use FD only for emergency fund or short-term parking.
🚫
Investing in Gold Jewellery
Making charges (15–25%) + GST + selling discount = massive loss on exit. Use Sovereign Gold Bonds (SGB) instead — 2.5% interest + gold price appreciation + no GST.
🚫
Timing the Market
"Time in the market beats timing the market." Missing the 10 best days of a 20-year market cycle can halve your returns. Stay invested always.
🚫
No Nomination / Will
Without nomination in mutual funds, bank accounts and insurance, your family will face legal battles for your own money. Update nominations today — takes 10 minutes.
