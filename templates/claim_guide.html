Two Types of Claims
Know which one applies to your situation
💳 Cashless Claim
✅
No upfront payment needed
Available only at network hospitals. The insurer directly pays the hospital — you pay nothing except non-covered amounts and copay.
Best for: Planned surgeries, elective procedures
Requires: Pre-authorization from insurer
Tip: Always verify hospital is in network before admission
💰 Reimbursement Claim
💸
You pay first, then claim back
Used when treated at non-network hospitals or in emergencies. You pay full bill, then submit claim with all original documents to get reimbursed.
Best for: Emergency admissions, non-network hospitals
Requires: All original bills and documents
Tip: Keep every receipt — no original = no reimbursement

Step-by-Step Claim Process
Follow these steps exactly to avoid rejection
1
Inform Your Insurer Immediately
Call your insurer's 24×7 helpline or TPA (Third Party Administrator) as soon as hospitalization is planned or occurs. For planned procedures, inform 3–5 days before. For emergencies, inform within 24 hours of admission.
⏱ Within 24 hrs (emergency) ⏱ 3–5 days before (planned) 💡 Save insurer helpline in phone
2
Show Your Health Card at Hospital
Present your health insurance card (physical or digital) to the hospital's insurance desk. They will verify your policy details and contact the insurer or TPA for authorization.
📄 Health Insurance Card 📄 Photo ID Proof 💡 Keep digital copy on phone
3
Pre-Authorization (Cashless Only)
For cashless claims, the hospital submits a pre-authorization form to the insurer. The insurer approves the estimated amount. This approval is required before treatment begins for non-emergency cases.
⏱ Insurer responds in 1–4 hours 📄 Pre-auth form from hospital 💡 Emergency cashless — approval can come after treatment
4
Collect All Documents During Hospitalization
While admitted, collect and preserve every document — doctor's notes, investigation reports, prescriptions, discharge summary. Do not leave hospital without the discharge summary and all original bills.
📄 All original bills & receipts 📄 Doctor's notes & prescriptions 📄 Lab & test reports 💡 Ask for itemized bill — not just final amount
5
Get Discharge Summary
The discharge summary is the most critical document for any claim. It must include diagnosis, treatment given, medicines prescribed, and doctor's signature. Without this, no claim will be processed.
📄 Discharge Summary (signed) ⏱ Get before leaving hospital 💡 Check diagnosis matches what was treated
6
Submit Claim Form + Documents
Fill the claim form accurately (download from insurer's website or get from hospital). Attach all original documents. Submit within the deadline — usually 15–30 days from discharge. Late submission = rejection.
⏱ Submit within 15–30 days 📄 Duly filled claim form 💡 Submit via registered post or insurer portal — keep proof
7
Track Your Claim
Use the insurer's mobile app, website or TPA portal to track claim status. Note your claim reference number. Follow up if no response in 7 days. IRDAI mandates settlement within 30 days of receiving all documents.
⏱ Settlement in 30 days (IRDAI mandate) 💡 Follow up every 7 days if no response
8
Receive Settlement or Reply to Query
Insurer will either settle the claim, partially approve it, or raise a query asking for additional documents. Respond to queries immediately with the requested documents. If rejected, you have the right to appeal.
💡 Keep copy of all submitted documents ⏱ Reply to queries within 7 days

What Gets Accepted vs Rejected
Know before you file
✅ Commonly Accepted Claims
🏥
Hospitalization (24+ hours)
Any illness or injury requiring 24+ hour admission is typically covered — surgeries, infections, accidents, organ failure etc.
🔪
Surgeries & Procedures
Appendectomy, bypass, knee replacement etc. are covered if medically necessary and not listed as exclusions.
🚑
Emergency Hospitalizations
Accidents, heart attacks, strokes — emergency admissions are covered even without prior intimation (notify within 24 hrs).
🔬
Day Care Procedures
Modern plans cover 200+ day care procedures like dialysis, cataract, chemotherapy that don't need 24hr admission.
🏠
Pre & Post Hospitalization
Medical expenses 30–60 days before admission and 60–90 days after discharge are covered in most policies.
🚐
Ambulance Charges
Road ambulance charges are covered up to a limit (usually ₹1,000–₹5,000) in most health insurance policies.
🚫 Top 12 Reasons for Rejection
🤫
Non-Disclosure of Pre-existing Disease
Hiding diabetes, BP, heart disease at time of buying policy. Insurer will reject any claim related to undisclosed conditions.
⏰
Waiting Period Not Over
Claiming for a disease within the waiting period (30 days initial / 2–4 yrs PED). Policy is clear — no coverage during this time.
📅
Late Submission of Claim
Missing the submission deadline (usually 15–30 days from discharge). Insurers strictly reject late submissions.
📄
Incomplete / Missing Documents
Missing original bills, no discharge summary, photocopies instead of originals. Every missing document is a rejection risk.
🚫
Excluded Treatment / Condition
Cosmetic surgery, dental treatment, infertility treatment, self-inflicted injuries — these are standard exclusions in all policies.
🏨
Less than 24 Hours Hospitalization
Old policies required 24hr admission. Unless your procedure is in the day care list, under-24hr stays are rejected.
💊
OPD Treatment Without Coverage
Doctor consultations, outpatient treatment and medicines without OPD rider are not covered in standard plans.
🍺
Alcohol / Drug Related Illness
Hospitalizations caused by alcohol or substance abuse are explicitly excluded in almost all health insurance policies.
📝
Wrong / Incorrect Claim Form
Errors in policy number, diagnosis code, dates, or unsigned forms lead to automatic rejection without investigation.
🏥
Non-Registered / Unrecognized Hospital
Treatment at hospitals not recognized by the government or not in the insurer's approved list may be rejected.
😷
Policy Lapsed / Premium Unpaid
If your policy lapsed due to missed premium and you claim during the lapse period, the entire claim will be rejected.
🔍
Suspected Fraud / Inflated Bills
Fraudulent claims, duplicate bills, or inflated bills are investigated and rejected. Can also lead to policy termination.

Claim Settlement Timeline
What happens when — IRDAI mandated deadlines
⏱ IRDAI Mandated Timelines
Day 0
Hospitalization / Discharge
Your treatment is complete. Start collecting all documents immediately before leaving the hospital.
Day 1–7
Inform Insurer & Prepare Documents
Notify insurer (if not already done). Organize all bills, reports, discharge summary and fill claim form.
Day 15–30
Submit Claim (Deadline)
Most insurers require submission within 15–30 days of discharge. Check your policy document for exact deadline.
Day 30
IRDAI Settlement Deadline
Insurer must settle the claim within 30 days of receiving all complete documents. If not, they must pay 2% interest per month on the pending amount.
Day 45
If No Settlement — Escalate to IRDAI
If claim is not settled or rejected without valid reason after 45 days, you can file a complaint with IRDAI's Bima Bharosa portal or approach the Insurance Ombudsman.

Pro Tips to Get Your Claim Approved
What experienced policyholders do differently
📸
Photograph Everything
Before submitting, photograph every document. Keep digital copies on Google Drive or phone. If originals are lost in transit, copies help you reconstruct the claim.
🔢
Get Itemized Bills Always
Never accept a single total bill. Ask for itemized bills showing each medicine, procedure, room charge separately. Insurers scrutinize vague bills and often reject them.
📞
Call TPA Before Discharge
Don't leave hospital without calling your TPA to confirm all documents are in order. Many claims fail simply because one document was missed at discharge time.
✍️
Fill Forms in Capital Letters
Always fill claim forms in block capital letters with a black pen. Messy handwriting, corrections with whitener, or missing signatures are common rejection reasons.
📮
Submit via Registered Post
If submitting physical documents, always use Speed Post / Registered Post. Keep the tracking receipt as proof of submission date — critical if there's a deadline dispute.
🏥
Always Check Network Before Admission
For planned procedures, confirm the hospital is in your insurer's network list. Cashless is denied if hospital is not in the empaneled list.
💬
Don't Exaggerate Symptoms
Never ask doctors to write inflated diagnoses to get a larger claim. Insurers cross-check with medical records and this can lead to fraud investigation and policy termination.
📖
Read Your Policy Once a Year
Most people never read their policy document. Read it once — know your sum insured, exclusions, waiting periods, sub-limits and co-pay. Surprises at claim time are avoidable.

If Your Claim is Rejected — Escalation Path
You have rights — here's how to use them
⚡ Step-by-Step Escalation
⚠️ Know Your Rights First
Every policyholder has the right to appeal a rejected claim. IRDAI protects you. Insurers MUST provide written reasons for rejection. You have up to 3 years to file a complaint. Interest is payable on delayed settlements.
1
Internal Grievance: Write to insurer's Grievance Redressal Officer (GRO). They must respond within 15 days. Ask for written rejection reason.
2
IRDAI Bima Bharosa: File online complaint at bimabharosa.irdai.gov.in or call 155255. IRDAI will mediate with the insurer on your behalf.
3
Insurance Ombudsman: For claims up to ₹30 lakhs, approach the nearest Insurance Ombudsman office. Free of cost. Decision is binding on insurer.
4
Consumer Forum: File a case at District Consumer Disputes Redressal Forum. Can claim compensation for mental harassment in addition to claim amount.
5
Civil Court: Last resort for large claim amounts. Engage a lawyer specializing in insurance disputes. Success rate is high if documentation is complete.
